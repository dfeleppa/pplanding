Daycare

## Body excerpt (verbatim)

Regularly attending an interactive doggy daycare can benefit your dog's health and wellbeing. Your dog can better their social skills, control their weight, help lessen separation anxiety, and lift their confidence, all just by playing with friends!

We strive to make daycare more than just a drop-off point; our goal is to create a space where your dog feels as relaxed and at home as they do with you.

Your dog's first day at Planet Pooch will be both fun and exciting! While adjusting to a new environment can take some time, our experienced staff is here to help your pooch settle in. With a consistent routine, your dog will soon be making friends and feeling right at home!

We offer two styles of daycare: **Group Play** and **Enrichment**. Group play might not be for every dog. If you find your dog does better individually, we also offer one-on-one **Enrichment Play**.

### Preparing for Daycare

**New Client Enrollment Packet/Evaluation** — Packet and evaluation completed before you can start daycare. We do a one time free meet and greet to ensure your pet isn't aggressive with other dogs and people. Meet and greet can take anywhere from 1 to 2 hours to get to know your pet's behavior. They can stay the rest of the day for free, as long as they pass evaluation.

### Checking In/Checking Out

Check-ins are available throughout the day, except during our closed period between 12:30pm-1:30pm (dogs rest period). We offer two sessions:
- **Morning session** (7am-12:30pm)
- **Full day session** (7am-7pm)

### Vaccines

Proof of Rabies, Distemper (DAPP/DHPP or DA2PP) and Bordetella vaccinations — All pets are required to be up to date every time you drop your dog off for daycare. We highly recommend getting your dog vaccinated for Influenza. Please provide updated vaccine records when the date expires. If you plan to have your dog vaccinated for Distemper (DAPP/DHPP or DA2PP) or Bordetella (Canine Cough), there will be a waiting period of 7 days before your dog can return to daycare.
